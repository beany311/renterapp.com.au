The problem

The system was built for landlords.
Not for you.

Until now, there has never been a dedicated tool built entirely for the renting side of the equation. Every platform serves the whole market. Landlords, agents, and renters. Renter fills that gap.

New laws in 2025 gave renters more rights than ever. No-grounds evictions banned. Rent caps introduced. Minimum property standards mandated. Most renters have no idea how to use them.

Maintenance ignored for months
No formal paper trail, no leverage. Most renters don't know they have legal recourse.
Bond withheld unfairly
$2,000–$5,000 gone, with no guidance on how to dispute it through VCAT or NCAT.
Applications keep getting rejected
No feedback, no idea what agents actually want. 10+ applications, zero approvals.
Rights exist but nobody explains them
State laws differ. Most renters can't afford a solicitor. Legal Aid is overloaded.

Why Renter exists

Built for one side only.

Every property platform in Australia serves landlords, agents, and renters. That means when interests conflict, renters lose.

Renter works for one side only. No landlord tools, no agent dashboards, no competing priorities. When we build something, we ask one question: does this help the person renting? If not, we don't build it.
